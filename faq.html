Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Seventh-day Adventists and our beliefs.

Why do Seventh-day Adventists worship on Saturday?

We observe the seventh-day Sabbath (Saturday) as commanded in the Bible. In Exodus 20:8-11, God instructs us to remember the Sabbath day and keep it holy. Jesus Himself observed the Sabbath, and the early Christian church continued this practice until the fourth century when Sunday observance became widespread.

Do Adventists celebrate Christmas and other holidays?

Many Adventists do celebrate Christmas as the birth of Jesus Christ, though we focus more on the spiritual significance than on secular traditions. We do not celebrate Easter with eggs and bunnies, but we do commemorate Christ's resurrection. We avoid Halloween due to its pagan origins.

What do Adventists believe about salvation?

We believe salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ alone. Ephesians 2:8-9 states, "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast." Good works are the fruit of salvation, not the means to it.

Why do many Adventists follow a vegetarian diet?

Our health message is based on biblical principles. Genesis 1:29 shows God's original diet was plant-based. We believe caring for our bodies honors God and helps us serve Him better. While vegetarianism is encouraged, it's not required for membership.

What is the "Adventist health message"?

Our health message includes eight natural remedies: pure air, sunlight, temperance, rest, exercise, proper diet, water, and trust in God. We operate hospitals and promote wellness through lifestyle choices that benefit both body and spirit.

Do Adventists believe in the Trinity?

Yes, we believe in one God who eternally exists as three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. This is clearly taught in Scripture (Matthew 28:19, 2 Corinthians 13:14).

What is the state of the dead according to Adventists?

We believe that when people die, they remain unconscious until the resurrection. Ecclesiastes 9:5-6 states that the dead know nothing. This is different from beliefs in heaven or hell immediately after death.

Why do Adventists have so many schools and hospitals?

Education and health are part of our mission to serve humanity. Jesus healed the sick and taught the people, so we follow His example by providing quality education and compassionate healthcare worldwide.

What is the remnant church?

In Revelation 12:17, God calls a remnant people who keep His commandments and have the testimony of Jesus. We believe this refers to faithful Christians in the last days who proclaim God's final message to the world.

How do Adventists view Ellen G. White?

We regard Ellen G. White as a prophet whose writings provide guidance and insight, but not as equal to Scripture. Her writings must be tested by the Bible (1 John 4:1). She never claimed infallibility and encouraged people to study the Bible for themselves.
